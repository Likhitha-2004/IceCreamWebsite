Comparison of Dairy and Nondairy Ice Cream Ice Creams made from cream or butter (dairy ice creams) and from vegetable fat (nondairy ice creams) are essentially comparable in nutritional value.

There are differences, however, in the vitamin A content, which is higher in dairy ice cream, and the vitamin E content, which is higher in nondairy ice cream.

There are also differences in the proportion of saturated and unsaturated fats present. Ice cream made from palm oil is lower in saturated fat than dairy ice cream, whereas ice cream made with coconut oil has a higher saturated fat content than a butterfat product.
